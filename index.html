What is a Wave?

A wave is a disturbance that is transmitted progressivly from one place to the next with no actual transport of matter.

A Wave

Crest- The peak of a wave

Trough- The low points of a wave

Wavelength- The distance between two crests or troughs

Amplitude- The distance between the midpoint and crest of a wave

Frequency- Amount of vibrations and object makes within a given time

Hertz- The unit of frequency

Types of Waves

Transverse Wave:

Motion of the medium that is at right angles to the direction in which a wave travels.

Longitudinal Waves:

Particles oscillating parallel to the direction of the wave.

What is a Sound?

Sound is vibrations that move through the air as a compressed wave.

Sound Frequency

Sounds have large range of frequencies. Some sounds are below or above our range of hearing. Humans can hear sounds from 20 hz to 20,000 hz.
